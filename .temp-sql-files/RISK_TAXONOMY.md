# Risk Taxonomy - Categories and Subcategories

**MinRisk Platform**
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-25

---

## Overview

This document outlines the standard risk taxonomy used in the MinRisk platform. Organizations can use this as a foundation and customize it to their specific needs through the Taxonomy Management interface.

---

## Additional Industry-Specific Categories

Organizations may add their own industry-specific categories such as:

- **Healthcare**: Clinical Risk, Patient Safety, Medical Device Risk
- **Financial Services**: Market Risk, Credit Risk, Liquidity Risk, Model Risk
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Drug Development Risk, Clinical Trial Risk, Regulatory Approval Risk
- **Manufacturing**: Production Risk, Equipment Risk, Maintenance Risk
- **Energy**: Exploration Risk, Refining Risk, Distribution Risk
- **Insurance**: Underwriting Risk, Claims Risk, Actuarial Risk
- **Real Estate**: Property Risk, Construction Risk, Tenant Risk

## How to Use This Taxonomy

1. **Select Appropriate Categories**: Choose the risk categories most relevant to your organization
2. **Customize Subcategories**: Add, modify, or remove subcategories based on your specific needs
3. **Assign Risks**: When creating risks, assign them to the appropriate category and subcategory
4. **Review Regularly**: Update the taxonomy annually or when your business changes significantly
5. **Maintain Consistency**: Ensure all users apply the taxonomy consistently across the organization

---

## Taxonomy Management

Administrators can manage the risk taxonomy through:
- **Web Interface**: Settings > Taxonomy Management
- **Bulk Import**: Upload Excel files with categories and subcategories
- **API**: Programmatic management via REST API

---

## Best Practices

✅ **DO:**
- Keep category names clear and distinct
- Limit subcategories to 10-15 per category
- Use business-friendly language
- Align with industry standards where applicable
- Document any custom categories

❌ **DON'T:**
- Create overlapping categories
- Use overly technical jargon
- Create too many levels of hierarchy
- Change taxonomy frequently without communication
